German Shepherd

The German Shepherd or German Shepherd Dog,[2][a] also known as the Alsatian, [3] is a German breed of working dog of medium to large size. It is a modern breed, developed by Max von Stephanitz from various traditional German herding dogs and wolf-dogs from 1899.[4][5]

It was originally bred as a herding dog, for herding sheep. It has since been used in many other types of work, including disability assistance, search-and-rescue, police work and warfare.[6][failed verification] It is commonly kept as a companion dog, and according to the Fédération Cynologique Internationale has the second-highest number of annual registrations.[7]
